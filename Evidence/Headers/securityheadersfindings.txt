Findings:

1. Missing Strict-Transport-Security Header
Risk Level: Medium

Description: HSTS ensures browsers communicate with the website only over HTTPS and prevents protocol downgrade attacks.

Recommendation: Implement the Strict-Transport-Security header with an appropriate max-age value and include subdomains where applicable.

2. Missing Content-Security-Policy Header
Risk Level: High

Description: A Content Security Policy helps reduce the risk of Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) by restricting which resources a browser is allowed to load.

Recommendation: Implement a strict CSP that allows only trusted sources.

3. Missing X-Frame-Options Header
Risk Level: Medium

Description: Without this header, the website may be vulnerable to clickjacking attacks through malicious framing.

Recommendation: Use X-Frame-Options: SAMEORIGIN or implement an equivalent CSP frame-ancestors directive.

4. Missing X-Content-Type-Options Header
Risk Level: Low

Description: Without this header, browsers may perform MIME-type sniffing, which can lead to unexpected content interpretation.

Recommendation: Configure X-Content-Type-Options: nosniff.

5. Missing Referrer-Policy Header
Risk Level: Low

Description: A missing Referrer Policy may result in unnecessary information being shared when users navigate away from the site.

Recommendation: Configure an appropriate Referrer-Policy based on privacy requirements.

6. Missing Permissions-Policy Header
Risk Level: Low

Description: Without a Permissions Policy, browser APIs and features may be available with broader permissions than required.

Recommendation: Define a Permissions-Policy to restrict unnecessary browser capabilities.
